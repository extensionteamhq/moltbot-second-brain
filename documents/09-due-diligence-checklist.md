# Due Diligence Checklist for Vacant Land

*Research compiled: February 10, 2026*

---

## Overview

Due diligence is the difference between a profitable flip and an expensive lesson. This checklist covers everything to verify before buying vacant land.

**Golden Rule:** Never skip due diligence. The 30-60 minutes spent checking these items can save thousands in bad deals.

---

## Master Checklist

### ☐ 1. Zoning & Permitted Uses

**What to check:**
- Zoning classification (residential, commercial, agricultural, etc.)
- Permitted uses under that zoning
- Conditional use requirements
- Setback and height requirements
- Minimum lot size requirements

**How to check:**
- Call local planning & zoning department with parcel number
- Search "[City/Township] Zoning Map" online
- Request zoning ordinance document
- Land Portal and land.id show general zoning (verify with county)

**Red flags:**
- Unusable zoning for intended purpose
- Property too small for required setbacks
- Overlay districts with extra restrictions

---

### ☐ 2. Title & Ownership Verification

**What to check:**
- Clear chain of ownership
- No liens (tax, mechanic's, judgment)
- No undisclosed easements
- Seller actually owns the property
- No boundary disputes

**How to check:**
- Order title search from title company ($150-300)
- Check county recorder's office online
- Pull deed copies
- For cheap properties: DIY title search using county records

**Red flags:**
- Multiple owners (requires all signatures)
- Recent ownership transfers (potential fraud)
- Outstanding liens exceeding property value
- Breaks in chain of title

---

### ☐ 3. Back Taxes & Assessments

**What to check:**
- Current tax status (paid/delinquent)
- Amount of any back taxes owed
- Special assessments (roads, utilities, HOA)
- Annual property tax amount

**How to check:**
- Search "[City/Township] Property Taxes" + parcel number
- Call county treasurer/tax collector
- Land Portal shows tax data

**Red flags:**
- Back taxes exceeding property value
- Tax sale pending
- Annual taxes disproportionately high (>4% of value)
- Unknown special assessments

**Reasonable annual tax:** 1-4% of full market value

---

### ☐ 4. Topography & Terrain

**What to check:**
- Slope and grade (affects buildability)
- Hills, ravines, cliffs
- Drainage patterns
- Rock formations

**How to check:**
- Google Earth (tilt view for 3D terrain)
- Land Portal topographic overlay
- USGS topo maps (free)
- Earth Point topo overlays
- Site visit (ideal but not always possible)

**Red flags:**
- Severe slopes (>25% grade)
- Property in a ravine or gully
- Poor drainage (ponding areas)
- Rocky terrain requiring blasting

---

### ☐ 5. Flood Zone Status

**What to check:**
- FEMA flood zone designation
- 100-year and 500-year flood plains
- Flood insurance requirements
- Historical flooding

**How to check:**
- FEMA.gov flood map service
- NFHL Viewer (National Flood Hazard Layer)
- Land Portal / land.id flood overlays
- County GIS portal

**Red flags:**
- Zone A or AE (high risk, insurance required)
- Zone VE (coastal high hazard)
- Any portion in floodway (no building allowed)

**Acceptable:** Zone X (minimal flood risk)

---

### ☐ 6. Legal Access & Road Frontage

**What to check:**
- Legal road access (public or private)
- Easement documentation
- Road maintenance agreements
- Year-round vs seasonal access
- Road condition

**How to check:**
- Review deed for easement language
- Check county GIS for road classifications
- Pull recorded easement documents
- Google Street View for road condition
- Call county roads department

**Red flags:**
- Landlocked (no legal access = major value hit)
- Easement requires neighbor permission
- Private road with no maintenance agreement
- Unimproved roads impassable in bad weather

---

### ☐ 7. Utilities Availability

**What to check:**
- Electric (distance to nearest pole)
- Water (municipal or well required)
- Sewer (municipal or septic required)
- Gas (if applicable)
- Internet/phone

**How to check:**
- Contact local utility providers
- Check for utility poles/meters on site (Google Street View)
- County planning department utility maps
- Land Portal utility data (verify with providers)

**Red flags:**
- Electric more than 1/4 mile away (extension costs $20-50/ft)
- No well water potential (arid areas)
- Failed perc tests on record
- No cell service in area

**Cost estimates:**
- Electric extension: $20-50 per linear foot
- Well drilling: $15-30 per foot (varies by region)
- Septic system: $3,000-15,000 depending on type

---

### ☐ 8. Perc Test & Soil Quality

**What to check:**
- Percolation test results (for septic eligibility)
- Soil type and drainage capacity
- Previous perc test records
- Engineered septic options if conventional fails

**How to check:**
- County health department records
- Ask seller for existing perc test
- Order new perc test ($300-500)
- Check soil maps (USDA Web Soil Survey)

**Red flags:**
- Failed perc test with no alternatives
- High water table
- Clay soil with poor drainage
- Bedrock close to surface

---

### ☐ 9. Environmental Issues

**What to check:**
- Wetlands or protected waters
- Endangered species habitats
- Contamination history
- Underground storage tanks
- Previous industrial/agricultural use

**How to check:**
- National Wetlands Inventory (NWI) maps
- EPA environmental databases
- Phase I ESA for higher-value properties
- County environmental health records
- Aerial photo history

**Red flags:**
- Wetlands covering buildable area
- EPA Superfund site nearby
- Former gas station/industrial use
- Pesticide contamination (former orchards)

**Cost:** Phase I ESA: $1,500-3,000 (usually only for $50K+ properties)

---

### ☐ 10. HOA & Deed Restrictions (CC&Rs)

**What to check:**
- Is property in an HOA?
- Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions document
- HOA fees and assessments
- Architectural review requirements
- Use restrictions (livestock, structures, etc.)

**How to check:**
- Deed documents reference CC&Rs
- Search subdivision name + HOA online
- Contact HOA management company
- Title company provides CC&Rs during closing

**Red flags:**
- High monthly HOA dues eating into profit
- Restrictions that limit buyer pool (no manufactured homes, etc.)
- HOA liens on property
- Architectural approval required (slows resale)

---

### ☐ 11. Property Boundaries & Survey

**What to check:**
- Accurate acreage
- Boundary locations
- Corner markers/monuments
- Encroachments from neighbors
- Fence line vs legal boundary

**How to check:**
- County GIS parcel viewer
- Existing survey on file
- Land Portal parcel overlay
- Site visit with GPS
- Order new survey if unclear

**Survey costs:**
- Boundary survey: $300-800
- ALTA survey (for lenders): $2,000-3,000+

**When to survey:**
- Boundary disputes
- Properties over $20K
- Required for title insurance
- Subdivision plans

---

### ☐ 12. Comparable Sales (Comps)

**What to check:**
- Recent sold prices for similar parcels
- Current listings (competition)
- Price per acre trends
- Days on market

**How to check:**
- Land Portal / PropStream comp reports
- LandWatch, Lands of America listings
- County assessor sales records
- Zillow (limited for land)

**Adjustments to make:**
- Size (price per acre decreases for larger parcels)
- Road access
- Utilities available
- Terrain/buildability
- Location within county

## Due Diligence Decision Framework

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Clear title, good access, buildable | ✅ Proceed |
| Minor issues (back taxes, small liens) | ⚠️ Negotiate price reduction |
| Major access or utility issues | ⚠️ Heavy discount or walk |
| Failed perc, wetlands, contamination | ❌ Walk unless extreme discount |
| Landlocked, no legal access | ❌ Walk (almost always) |

---

## Due Diligence Timeline

**For properties under $5K:**
- 1-2 days of desktop research
- Verify ownership, taxes, zoning, flood zone
- Skip formal title search if comfortable

**For properties $5K-$20K:**
- 3-5 days of thorough research
- Order title search or title insurance
- Verify all checklist items

**For properties over $20K:**
- Full due diligence period (7-14 days in contract)
- Professional title search + title insurance
- Consider survey if boundaries unclear
- Site visit if practical

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trusting seller information** — Always verify independently
2. **Skipping flood zone check** — Can make property uninsurable/unbuildable
3. **Assuming road access** — "Near a road" ≠ legal access
4. **Ignoring HOA restrictions** — Can kill resale to certain buyers
5. **Not checking back taxes** — Can exceed property value
6. **Skipping comp research** — Overpaying kills profit margins
7. **Rushing due diligence** — Cheap properties still need checks
