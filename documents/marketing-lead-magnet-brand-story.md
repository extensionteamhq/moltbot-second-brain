## Exercise 3.3: Your One-Liner

A one-liner is a memorized statement that clearly explains what you do. It follows a simple formula.

**Formula:** [Problem] + [Solution] + [Result]

**Template:**
"Most [target customers] struggle with [problem]. We [solution], so they can [result]."

**Examples:**
- "Most land sellers wait months and lose money to agents. We make cash offers in 48 hours, so they can move on without the hassle."
- "Most small businesses struggle to get consistent leads. We build automated marketing systems, so they can grow without guessing."

# Part 8: Apply Your Message

## Quick-Start Applications

Now that you have your framework, use it everywhere:

### Website Homepage Formula

1. **Header:** State what you offer + who it's for
2. **Stakes:** What failure looks like (the problem)
3. **Value:** What success looks like (the transformation)
4. **Guide:** Why they should trust you (empathy + authority)
5. **Plan:** Show them how it works (3-4 steps)
6. **CTA:** Direct call to action (make it obvious)
7. **Testimonials:** Proof from other heroes you've helped

### Email Sequence Formula

1. **Email 1:** Empathyâ€”"I understand your problem"
2. **Email 2:** Authorityâ€”"Here's why I can help"
3. **Email 3:** Valueâ€”"Here's what life looks like after"
4. **Email 4:** Planâ€”"Here's exactly how it works"
5. **Email 5:** CTAâ€”"Ready? Here's your next step"

### Elevator Pitch Formula

"You know how [hero] struggles with [problem]? Well, what I do is [solution], so they can [result]. In fact, [authority proof]."

## Checklist: Is Your Message Clear?

Before you launch, check your marketing against this list:

- [ ] Can someone understand what you offer in 5 seconds?
- [ ] Is it clear who this is for?
- [ ] Is the customer the hero (not you)?
- [ ] Have you addressed both external AND internal problems?
- [ ] Do you come across as both empathetic AND competent?
- [ ] Is there a clear, simple plan (3-4 steps max)?
- [ ] Is your call to action obvious and repeated?
- [ ] Have you painted success vividly?
- [ ] Have you hinted at the cost of inaction?
- [ ] Would your grandma understand it?
