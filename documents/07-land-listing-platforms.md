# Land Listing & Disposition Platforms

*Research completed: February 8, 2026 (2am ET research window)*

---

## Overview

When you're ready to sell land (disposition phase), choosing the right platforms determines how fast you find buyers. This guide covers the best platforms for land flippers, organized by priority.

---

## Tier 1: Land-Specialty Portals (Primary)

These platforms attract buyers specifically searching for land—warmer leads, faster conversions.

### Land.com Network (The Core Stack)

The Land.com Network includes three interconnected platforms owned by the same company:

| Platform | Focus | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **LandWatch** | Largest rural land marketplace | 30+ years in market, highest traffic |
| **Lands of America** | Farms, ranches, rural properties | Strong in agricultural markets |
| **Land And Farm** | Agricultural/farming focus | Soil/water rights filters |

**Key Benefits:**
- Cross-listing: Post on one, syndicated to all three
- Targeted audience actively filtering by acreage, access, utilities, price
- Options for free basic listings + paid featured placements
- Unlimited photos, video, maps, and detailed land info

**Pricing Structure:**
- Basic listings: Free (for individual sellers/FSBO)
- Featured/Premium placements: Paid (increases visibility)
- Broker/Agent plans: Monthly subscription for multiple listings

**Best For:** Rural/recreational land, 5+ acres, areas outside metros

## Tier 2: Broad Marketplaces (Supplemental)

Add these for extra reach and local interest.

### Facebook Marketplace

**Pros:**
- Free to list
- Built-in local audience
- Organic sharing potential
- Direct messaging with buyers
- Good for cash buyers and neighbor inquiries

**Cons:**
- Attracts casual browsers (more tire-kickers)
- High inquiry volume to sort through
- Not specialized for land

**Best For:** Quick local interest, cash buyers, smaller parcels

### Craigslist

**Pros:**
- Free
- Local reach
- Cash buyer friendly

**Cons:**
- More spam/scam inquiries
- Less sophisticated buyers

**Best For:** Local cash buyers, supplement to main platforms

### Zillow

**Pros:**
- Massive audience (36M+ monthly visitors)
- Free listing option
- High visibility

**Cons:**
- Geared toward home sales, not land
- Serious land buyers may prefer specialized sites

**Best For:** Buildable lots near towns, residential-adjacent parcels

---

## Tier 3: MLS & Agent Networks

### MLS (Multiple Listing Service)

Access via real estate agent or flat-fee MLS service.

**Pros:**
- Feeds to third-party portals (Zillow, Redfin, Realtor.com)
- Attracts financed buyers
- Creates appraiser-friendly comps

**Cons:**
- Requires agent or flat-fee service
- Commission costs (5-10% typical for land)

**When to Use:**
- You need lender-friendly comps
- Targeting financed buyers
- Infill/buildable lots near towns

### Flat-Fee MLS Services

Pay a flat fee ($100-500) to get on MLS without full agent commission.

## Tier 4: Specialized/Niche Platforms

### LoopNet

**Best For:** Commercial land, development parcels, industrial sites

### Whitetail Properties

Specialized brokerage for hunting/recreational land. Higher commissions but deep expertise and targeted buyer network.

**Best For:** Hunting land, recreational tracts, premium rural acreage

## Listing Asset Checklist

High-quality listings convert. Include these:

### Must-Have Assets
- [ ] Parcel outline map (labeled)
- [ ] GPS pins (entrance + center)
- [ ] Approach/road photos
- [ ] Frontage/corners photos
- [ ] Context map (nearest paved road, power, water, route from highway)

### Highly Recommended
- [ ] Recent survey/plat (if available)
- [ ] Utility notes (nearest power/water, well depth norms, perc/septic status)
- [ ] Zoning/use-by-right bullets
- [ ] Flood/soils pre-screens
- [ ] Clear driving directions (highway exit → mile markers → landmarks)
- [ ] Short video walk-through (60-90 seconds)

### Pro Tips
- Lead with certainty signals in title: "[Acres] + [Access/Utility Signal] + [Use/Feature] + [Distance to Town]"
- Example: "5.2 acres with legal access, power nearby, perc-tested, 18 min to Asheville"
- Answer top 5 questions in first paragraph: access, utilities, zoning, restrictions, directions
- Label photos: "North corner," "Nearest pole," "Drainage swale"
- Horizontal video, stabilized, talk like guiding a friend

## Disposition Workflow for Dirt Roamers

### Phase 1: Prep (Before Listing)
1. Gather all property docs (deed, survey, tax records)
2. Create asset package (maps, photos, GPS, disclosures)
3. Determine pricing (comps, desired margin, terms options)
4. Decide: cash only vs. seller financing available

### Phase 2: Launch (Day 1)
1. Post to Land.com Network (LandWatch primary)
2. Post to Facebook Marketplace
3. Post to Craigslist (local area)
4. Optional: Flat-fee MLS if targeting financed buyers

### Phase 3: Optimize (Days 4-10)
1. Review inquiry quality
2. Test headline variations
3. Improve photo order
4. Consider adding owner-finance terms

### Phase 4: Adjust (Days 11-14)
1. If inquiries lag: improve photos/headline first
2. Then test terms (owner financing)
3. Then consider small price adjustment
4. Add premium/featured boost if high-competition area

## Key Takeaways for Dirt Roamers

1. **Core Stack:** LandWatch + Facebook Marketplace + Craigslist = solid free coverage
2. **Asset Quality > Platform Choice:** Great photos/maps/disclosures matter more than which site
3. **Seller Financing = Wider Buyer Pool:** Offering terms can justify full asking price
4. **Speed Tactic:** Post everywhere Day 1, optimize based on response data
5. **Track Metrics:** Note where each inquiry comes from to optimize future listings
