# Building Your Land Buyer List

*Created: February 16, 2026*

---

## Why Your Buyer List Is Everything

The #1 predictor of success in land flipping: **how fast you can sell**.

A strong buyer list means:
- Faster closings (days, not months)
- Higher prices (competitive buyers)
- Less marketing spend (direct outreach)
- Repeat business (investors buy multiple times)

**Goal:** Build a qualified list of 25-50 active buyers BEFORE you have deals to sell.

---

## Types of Land Buyers

### Tier 1: Active Investors (Your VIPs)

| Buyer Type | Characteristics | Buying Pattern |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Land flippers** | Buy cheap, sell retail | 5-20 parcels/year |
| **Developers** | Buy for subdivision/building | High volume, specific areas |
| **Builders** | Need lots for new construction | Consistent demand |
| **Hedge funds/LLCs** | Institutional buyers | Large volume, quick close |

**These buyers:**
- Close fast (often cash)
- Buy repeatedly
- Refer other buyers
- Pay wholesale prices (but reliably)

### Tier 2: End Users

| Buyer Type | Characteristics | Buying Pattern |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Dream home builders** | Want land to build on | One-time, emotional |
| **Recreational buyers** | Hunting, camping, retreat | Seasonal, price-sensitive |
| **Homesteaders** | Self-sufficient living | Specific requirements |
| **Neighbors** | Expand existing property | Highest prices, slow |

**These buyers:**
- Pay retail or above
- Take longer to close
- Often need financing (seller finance opportunity)
- One-time purchases

### Tier 3: Opportunistic

| Buyer Type | Characteristics | Buying Pattern |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **"Pickup truck investors"** | Mom-and-pop flippers | Occasional, local |
| **Speculators** | Buy and hold for appreciation | Slow, patient |
| **Tax buyers** | Look for tax deed opportunities | Niche, specific |

## 10 Ways to Find Cash Buyers

### 1. PropWire (Highly Recommended)

**What:** Database of property transactions with buyer/seller info

**How to use:**
1. Search your target county
2. Filter: "Lead Type" â†’ Cash Buyers
3. Filter: "Property Type" â†’ Vacant Land
4. Filter: "Owner Type" â†’ Businesses (LLCs, Corps)

**Gold mine feature:** Click any buyer to see ALL properties they own nationwide. If they own 100+ land parcels, they're a serious buyer.

**Cost:** ~$99/month

### 2. County Recorder / Assessor Records

**What:** Public records of all property sales

**How to use:**
1. Go to county assessor website or visit office
2. Search deed transfers for last 12 months
3. Filter for vacant land sales
4. Extract buyer names/entities
5. Skip trace for contact info

**Pro tip:** Look for repeat buyers (same LLC name appearing multiple times)

**Cost:** Free to cheap

### 3. Facebook Groups

**Best groups for land buyers:**
- "Land Investors" (largest)
- "Vacant Land Investing"
- "[State] Land Investors" (state-specific)
- "Raw Land Flipping"
- "Creative Finance for Real Estate"

**Strategy:**
1. Join groups, be active (not spammy)
2. When you have a deal, post: "Just locked up [X] acres in [County], looking for buyer. DM for details"
3. Collect contact info from everyone who responds
4. Add to buyer list (even if they don't buy this one)

**Cost:** Free

### 4. Land Listing Platforms (Reverse Search)

**Where:**
- LandWatch
- Lands of America
- Land.com
- Zillow Lots/Land

**How to use:**
1. Search for SOLD listings in your market
2. Note the agents/companies selling
3. Contact them: "I wholesale land in [area], want to be notified of my deals?"
4. Agents often have buyer lists themselves

**Cost:** Free

### 5. BiggerPockets Forums

**Strategy:**
1. Post in "Land Investing" or "Wholesaling" forums
2. Share market insights (builds credibility)
3. When you have deals, post: "Deal Alert: [brief details]"
4. Collect interested parties

**Bonus:** Connect with investors in specific markets through marketplace posts

**Cost:** Free (or Pro membership ~$390/year for more features)

### 6. Builder Outreach

**Who to target:**
- National builders: DR Horton, Lennar, KB Home, Meritage
- Regional builders in your market
- Custom home builders (Google "[city] custom home builder")

**How to reach:**
1. Google "[builder name] land acquisition"
2. Find the land acquisition manager or purchasing director
3. Email/call: "I source land deals in [market]. Can I send you my inventory when I have deals?"

**Sample outreach:**
```
Subject: Land acquisition opportunities in [County]

Hi [Name],

I work with land owners in [County] who are looking to sell. I noticed [Builder] is active in the area.

Do you have a land acquisition team I could connect with when deals come across my desk?

I typically see [X-acre residential lots / infill parcels / etc.] and can send details when I have inventory.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Cost:** Free (just time)

### 7. REIA Meetings (Real Estate Investor Associations)

**Strategy:**
1. Find local REIA in your market (Google "[city] real estate investor association")
2. Attend meetings regularly
3. Network with: flippers, wholesalers, buy-and-hold investors
4. Collect cards, add to CRM, follow up

**What to say:**
"I focus on land deals in [area]. When I find good ones, I'd love to send them your way. What are you looking for?"

**Cost:** $100-300/year membership

### 8. Direct Mail to Land Owners (Neighbor Strategy)

**How it works:**
1. When you get a property under contract
2. Pull list of all adjacent landowners
3. Send postcard: "Your neighbor's property is for sale. Interested in expanding?"

**Why it works:** Neighbors pay premium prices (consolidation, privacy, access)

**Cost:** $0.50-1.50/piece

### 9. Craigslist & Facebook Marketplace

**Two strategies:**

**A) Post your deals**
- List properties in "Real Estate for Sale"
- Include: location, size, price, contact method
- Collect all inquiries (even non-buyers become leads)

**B) Respond to "Looking for land" posts**
- Search "looking for land" or "want to buy land"
- Reach out: "I source land in this area. What are you looking for?"
- Add to buyer list

**Cost:** Free

### 10. Your Existing Deals (Snowball Effect)

**Every deal generates more buyers:**
- Buyer who purchased â†’ ask for referrals
- Buyers who inquired but didn't buy â†’ keep on list
- Agents involved â†’ add to list
- Title company contacts â†’ often know active buyers

**Ask after every closing:**
"Do you know anyone else looking for land in this area? I often find good deals."

## Buyer Qualification Process

### Questions to Ask Every Potential Buyer

1. **What areas are you buying in?** (County/state specific)
2. **What size parcels?** (5-10 acres, 20+ acres, etc.)
3. **What's your typical budget per deal?** (Qualifying)
4. **How do you fund deals?** (Cash, hard money, seller finance)
5. **How quickly can you close?** (Days or weeks = serious)
6. **How many deals do you do per year?** (Volume buyer vs. one-time)
7. **What's a deal-breaker for you?** (Access issues, utilities, etc.)

### Scoring Your Buyers

| Score | Criteria | Priority |
|-------|----------|----------|
| A-Buyer | Cash, closes in 14 days, 5+ deals/year | Send deals first |
| B-Buyer | Cash or financing, 30-day close, 2-5 deals/year | Second wave |
| C-Buyer | Slower close, specific requirements, 1-2 deals/year | Nurture |

**Focus on building A and B buyers first.**

## Pro Tips

### 1. Quality > Quantity
25 A-buyers beats 500 unqualified contacts. Focus on people who actually close deals.

### 2. Buyers Want Deals, Not Spam
Only send when you have real inventory. Weekly max. Don't cry wolf.

### 3. Speed Wins
Text or call A-buyers before email blasts. First to respond often gets the deal.

### 4. Referrals Are Gold
After every closing: "Know anyone else looking for land?" One satisfied buyer can refer 5 more.

### 5. Keep It Warm
Touch your list monthly even without deals. Market updates, insights, check-ins. Cold lists don't convert.

### 6. Use Assignment Fees as Social Proof
"I just assigned a 10-acre parcel in [County] for $12K profit" attracts more buyers than "I have deals."
