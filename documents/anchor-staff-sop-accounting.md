# SOP: Accounting & Finance Process
## Anchor & Staff Agency

### Purpose
Maintain financial health through proper invoicing, expense management, reporting, and cash flow optimization.

---

## 1. Revenue Management

### Pricing Models
| Model | Best For | Structure |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Project-Based | One-time engagements | Fixed fee per deliverable |
| Retainer | Ongoing relationships | Monthly fee for scope of work |
| Hourly | Undefined scope | Rate Ã— hours (with cap) |
| Value-Based | High-impact projects | Priced on outcome value |

### Pricing Guidelines
- Revenue Roadmap: $3,000 - $7,500
- Brand Strategy: $5,000 - $15,000
- Website: $10,000 - $50,000+
- Monthly Retainer: $2,500 - $10,000+

### Payment Terms
- **Standard:** 50% upfront, 50% on completion
- **Retainer:** Monthly in advance
- **Large projects:** Milestone-based payments
- **Due date:** Net 15 or Net 30

## 2. Invoicing Process

### Invoice Creation
1. Generate invoice upon milestone/completion
2. Include: Client name, project, description, amount, due date
3. Add payment instructions (ACH preferred, credit card accepted)
4. Review for accuracy before sending
5. Send via email with professional cover note

### Invoice Components
- Invoice number (sequential)
- Invoice date
- Due date
- Client billing address
- Line item descriptions
- Subtotal, taxes (if applicable), total
- Payment methods accepted
- Late payment policy

### Invoice Schedule
| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Contract signed | Send deposit invoice |
| Milestone reached | Send milestone invoice |
| Project complete | Send final invoice |
| 1st of month | Send retainer invoice |

## 3. Collections Process

### Payment Follow-Up
| Days Overdue | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| Due date | Payment reminder email |
| 7 days | Friendly follow-up |
| 14 days | Phone call + email |
| 30 days | Formal notice, pause work |
| 60 days | Collections consideration |

### Follow-Up Templates

**7 Days:**
> Hi [Name], just a friendly reminder that invoice #[X] for $[Amount] was due on [Date]. Please let me know if you have any questions or need anything from us to process payment.

**14 Days:**
> Hi [Name], following up on invoice #[X] which is now 14 days past due. Is there an issue we should discuss? Please let us know how to proceed.

**30 Days:**
> Hi [Name], invoice #[X] is now 30 days overdue. Per our agreement, we'll need to pause work until payment is received. Please contact us immediately to resolve this.

## 4. Expense Management

### Expense Categories
- **Labor:** Salaries, contractors, benefits
- **Software:** Tools, subscriptions, licenses
- **Marketing:** Ads, content, events
- **Operations:** Office, equipment, supplies
- **Professional:** Legal, accounting, insurance
- **Travel:** Client meetings, conferences

### Expense Approval
| Amount | Approver |
|--------|----------|
| < $500 | Self-approval with receipt |
| $500 - $2,000 | Partner approval |
| > $2,000 | Both partners |

### Expense Tracking
- Capture receipts immediately (photo/scan)
- Categorize in accounting software
- Review monthly for accuracy
- Reconcile with bank statements

## 5. Financial Tools

### Recommended Stack
- **Accounting:** QuickBooks, Xero, or FreshBooks
- **Invoicing:** Built into accounting software
- **Payments:** Stripe, Square, or PayPal
- **Banking:** Mercury, Relay, or traditional bank
- **Expense Tracking:** Expensify or built into accounting
- **Payroll:** Gusto, Rippling, or ADP

### Bank Account Structure
- **Operating Account:** Day-to-day expenses
- **Tax Reserve:** Set aside 25-30% of profit
- **Profit Account:** Owner distributions
- **Emergency Fund:** 3-6 months expenses

---

## 6. Cash Flow Management

### Cash Flow Best Practices
- Invoice promptly (same day as milestone)
- Offer early payment discounts (2% Net 10)
- Require deposits on all new projects
- Monitor accounts receivable weekly
- Maintain cash reserve (3+ months expenses)

### Cash Flow Forecasting
- Project expected revenue (signed contracts + pipeline)
- Project expected expenses (fixed + variable)
- Identify gaps 30-60-90 days out
- Adjust spending or accelerate collections

### Red Flags
- AR > 30 days average
- Cash reserve < 2 months
- Revenue concentrated in few clients
- Expenses growing faster than revenue

---

## 7. Financial Reporting

### Monthly Reports
- **P&L Statement:** Revenue, expenses, profit
- **Cash Flow:** Money in vs. money out
- **AR Aging:** Outstanding invoices by age
- **Budget vs. Actual:** Performance against plan

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Gross margin | 50-70% |
| Net profit margin | 15-25% |
| AR days outstanding | < 30 days |
| Cash runway | 3+ months |
| Revenue per employee | $150K+ |

### Reporting Schedule
- Weekly: Cash position, AR status
- Monthly: P&L, cash flow, metrics
- Quarterly: Full financial review, forecasting
- Annually: Tax prep, strategic planning

---

## 8. Tax Management

### Tax Obligations
- **Income tax:** Quarterly estimated payments
- **Self-employment tax:** If applicable
- **Sales tax:** If services are taxable in state
- **Payroll tax:** If employees on payroll

### Tax Calendar
| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| Jan 31 | 1099s to contractors |
| Apr 15 | Q1 estimated taxes |
| Jun 15 | Q2 estimated taxes |
| Sep 15 | Q3 estimated taxes |
| Jan 15 | Q4 estimated taxes |
| Mar/Apr | Annual tax filing |

### Tax Best Practices
- Set aside 25-30% of profit for taxes
- Track deductible expenses carefully
- Work with qualified accountant/CPA
- Keep records for 7 years

---

## 9. Contractor Payments

### Contractor Setup
- W-9 on file before first payment
- Clear SOW with payment terms
- Track hours/deliverables

### Payment Process
1. Contractor submits invoice
2. Review against SOW/deliverables
3. Approve and schedule payment
4. Pay within agreed terms (Net 15-30)
5. Record in accounting system

### Year-End
- Issue 1099-NEC for contractors paid $600+
- Send by January 31

---

## 10. Financial Policies

### Pricing Changes
- Review pricing annually
- Existing clients: 30-day notice
- New clients: Immediate

### Refund Policy
- Deposit: Non-refundable (covers discovery work)
- Unused retainer: Prorated refund
- Disputes: Case-by-case, document everything

### Profit Distribution
- Partners decide distribution quarterly
- Retain minimum 20% for growth/reserves
- Tax implications considered
