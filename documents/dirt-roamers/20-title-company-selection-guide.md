# Title Company Selection Guide

*Last updated: February 18, 2026*

---

## Overview

Choosing the right title company is critical for land investors. You need closing agents who:
- **Understand investor transactions** (assignments, double closings, seller financing)
- **Handle rural land** (not just residential homes)
- **Communicate proactively** (don't let deals die in escrow)
- **Price competitively** (closing costs eat into margins on smaller deals)

This guide covers investor-friendly title companies and closing attorneys across Mateo's target markets: **AL, FL, GA, MT, NC, SC, TN**.

## What to Look For in a Closing Agent

### Must-Have Capabilities

| Capability | Why It Matters |
|------------|----------------|
| **Assignments** | Wholesale deals where you assign the contract to end buyer |
| **Double Closings** | Buy and sell same day (A→B→C) |
| **Single-Source Funding** | Use end buyer's funds for both transactions (reduces capital needed) |
| **Seller Financing Documents** | Promissory notes, deeds of trust, land contracts |
| **Proactive Communication** | Don't let deals die waiting on title work |
| **Title Issue Resolution** | Actively solve problems, not just report them |

### Nice-to-Have

| Feature | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| **Loan Servicing** | Same company can service your seller finance notes |
| **Investor Pricing** | Discounts for repeat business |
| **Remote Closing** | E-signatures, mail-away closings for remote land |
| **Multi-State Coverage** | One relationship for multiple markets |

## Building Your Network

### Step 1: Start with One per State

Don't try to build relationships with every company. Pick one recommended closing agent per state you're targeting:

| State | Recommended First Contact |
|-------|--------------------------|
| AL | Conwell Title & Escrow |
| FL | Gulfside Title Services |
| GA | Dixon Law Group |
| MT | Flathead Premier Title |
| NC | Brock & Scott PLLC |
| SC | Hopkins Law Firm |
| TN | Title Group of Tennessee (or HouseCashin search) |

### Step 2: Initial Conversation

Call and ask:
1. "Do you work with land investors who flip vacant land?"
2. "Can you handle assignments and double closings?"
3. "What's your fee structure for a typical vacant land transaction?"
4. "Do you have experience with seller financing documents?"
5. "What's your average turnaround time from contract to close?"

### Step 3: Test with a Deal

Send them your first deal. Evaluate:
- Communication speed and quality
- Problem-solving ability
- Final costs vs. quoted
- Overall experience

### Step 4: Document and Expand

After a successful closing:
- Add them to your "proven partners" list
- Ask for referrals to other counties/states
- Negotiate volume pricing if doing multiple deals

## Cost Expectations

### Typical Closing Costs (Land Transactions)

| Deal Size | Approach | Expected Cost |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Under $5K | Self-closing or hybrid | $50-300 |
| $5K-$15K | Title company (title insurance optional) | $300-700 |
| $15K-$50K | Full title company | $700-1,200 |
| $50K+ | Full title with enhanced coverage | $1,200-2,000+ |

### What's Included

- Title search
- Document preparation
- Escrow/settlement services
- Recording fees
- Wire fees

### What's Extra

- Title insurance (often optional for land, ~$500-1,500)
- Survey (if needed, $300-1,500)
- Attorney fees (in attorney states, add $200-500)

## Red Flags

Watch out for:

⚠️ **"We don't do land closings"** — Not familiar with vacant land nuances

⚠️ **"What's a double closing?"** — Not investor-friendly

⚠️ **Slow communication** — If they take 3+ days to respond during vetting, they'll be worse mid-transaction

⚠️ **Surprise fees** — Get itemized quote upfront

⚠️ **No problem-solving** — "That's a title issue, you'll need to figure that out" (vs. actively helping resolve)

⚠️ **Pushing unnecessary services** — Pressuring title insurance on every $3K deal
