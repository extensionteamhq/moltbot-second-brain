# Closing Options Guide for Land Transactions

*Research compiled: February 10, 2026*

---

## Overview

There are three main ways to close a vacant land transaction:

1. **Self-Closing** — You handle everything (cheapest, most work)
2. **Title Company / Attorney** — Professional handles it (safest, most expensive)
3. **Hybrid Approach** — Title insurance + DIY closing (middle ground)

The right choice depends on property value, risk tolerance, and deal economics.

---

## Decision Matrix

| Property Value | Recommended Approach | Typical Cost |
|---------------|---------------------|--------------|
| Under $5,000 | Self-Closing | $50-200 |
| $5,000 - $15,000 | Hybrid (title insurance + self-close) | $300-600 |
| $15,000 - $50,000 | Title Company | $500-1,500 |
| Over $50,000 | Title Company + Survey | $1,500-3,000+ |

**Key insight:** Self-closing only makes sense for cheap properties where closing costs would eat the profit margin.

## Option 1: Self-Closing

### When It Makes Sense
- Properties under $5,000
- Clear chain of title (verified yourself)
- Motivated to save on closing costs
- Comfortable with legal documents
- Non-attorney closing state (see state requirements below)

### What You'll Handle
1. **Title Search** — Verify ownership and liens yourself
2. **Purchase Agreement** — Draft or use template
3. **Deed Preparation** — Create the new deed
4. **Notarization** — Get signatures notarized
5. **Recording** — Submit deed to county recorder
6. **Funds Transfer** — Collect payment, distribute funds

### Self-Closing Costs
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| DIY title search | $0-50 (county fees) |
| Deed preparation | $0 (templates available) |
| Notary fees | $10-25 per signature |
| Recording fees | $15-100 (varies by county) |
| **Total** | **$50-200** |

### Self-Closing Process

**Step 1: Verify Title**
- Search county recorder for deed history
- Check for liens, judgments, easements
- Verify seller matches current owner of record

**Step 2: Prepare Documents**
- Purchase agreement (signed by both parties)
- New deed (warranty deed or quitclaim, depending on situation)
- Closing statement / settlement sheet

**Step 3: Execute Closing**
- Meet with seller (or use mobile notary)
- Collect signed, notarized deed
- Exchange funds (cashier's check or wire)
- Provide seller with signed closing statement

**Step 4: Record the Deed**
- Submit original deed to county recorder
- Pay recording fees
- Receive recorded deed back (usually mailed)

### Self-Closing Risks
- **No title insurance** — You're exposed if title issues emerge later
- **Missed liens** — DIY search might miss something
- **Document errors** — Incorrect deeds can cloud title
- **Legal exposure** — Mistakes could require attorney to fix

### Self-Closing Resources
- REtipster: "How to Close a Cash Transaction"
- County recorder websites for deed templates
- Mobile notary services (Notarize.com, Snapdocs)

## Option 2: Title Company / Attorney Closing

### When It Makes Sense
- Properties over $10,000
- Complex title history
- Multiple sellers or heirs
- Seller financing deals
- Out-of-state purchases
- Risk aversion / peace of mind

### What They Handle
- Full title search and examination
- Title insurance policy
- Document preparation
- Escrow services
- Notarization
- Recording
- Funds disbursement

### Title Company Costs
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Title search | $150-300 |
| Title insurance (owner's policy) | $200-500+ (based on purchase price) |
| Closing/escrow fee | $300-800 |
| Document preparation | Often included |
| Recording | $15-100 |
| Wire fees | $25-50 |
| **Total** | **$700-1,800** |

**Title insurance cost formula:** Typically $3-5 per $1,000 of purchase price (varies by state)

### Finding Land-Friendly Title Companies

Not all title companies are created equal. Many focus on residential homes and consider land deals a hassle.

**What to look for:**
- Experience with vacant land transactions
- Willingness to close lower-value deals
- Reasonable minimums (some won't do under $50K)
- Fast turnaround times
- Remote/mail-away closing capability

**How to find them:**
- Ask in land investing communities (Land Geek, REtipster forums)
- BiggerPockets forums for state-specific recommendations
- Call 5-10 companies and ask about land experience
- Real estate attorneys often know land-friendly title companies

**Questions to ask:**
1. Do you close vacant land transactions?
2. What's your minimum deal size?
3. What are your fees for a $[X] land purchase?
4. Can you do mail-away/remote closings?
5. What's your typical turnaround time?

## Option 3: Hybrid Approach

### When It Makes Sense
- Properties $5,000 - $15,000
- Want title insurance protection
- Comfortable handling logistics yourself
- Title company minimums too high for deal size

### How It Works
1. **Order title insurance only** from a title company
2. **Close the transaction yourself** (documents, notary, recording)
3. Get protection without full closing fees

### Hybrid Costs
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Title search + insurance | $300-500 |
| Document preparation (DIY) | $0 |
| Mobile notary | $25-100 |
| Recording | $15-100 |
| **Total** | **$350-700** |

### How to Order Title Insurance Only
- Call title companies and ask if they offer "title insurance only" without closing services
- Some title companies will issue a policy if you provide the signed deed
- Online title insurance providers: Old Republic, First American, Fidelity

## State-by-State Closing Requirements

Some states require attorneys for real estate closings. Others allow title companies or even self-closing.

### Attorney-Required States (for at least one party)
- Connecticut
- Delaware
- Georgia
- Massachusetts
- New York
- North Carolina
- South Carolina
- Vermont
- West Virginia

### Title Company States (no attorney required)
- Alabama ✓
- Florida ✓
- Montana ✓
- Tennessee ✓

**For Mateo's target markets:**

| State | Requirement | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Alabama | Title company OK | No attorney required |
| Florida | Title company OK | Very land-investor friendly |
| Georgia | Attorney typically involved | Some flexibility for land |
| Montana | Title company OK | Limited title company options in rural areas |
| North Carolina | Attorney required | Must use closing attorney |
| South Carolina | Attorney required | Must use closing attorney |
| Tennessee | Title company OK | No attorney required |

**Tip:** In attorney states, closing costs are higher. Factor this into offer prices.

## Seller Financing Closing Considerations

When selling with seller financing, the closing process is more complex:

### Additional Documents Needed
- Promissory note (loan terms)
- Deed of trust or mortgage (security instrument)
- Payment schedule/amortization
- Default and remedies language

### Recommended Approach
- Use a title company or attorney for seller finance deals
- Proper recording of security instrument is critical
- Consider using a loan servicing company to collect payments

### Loan Servicing Options
| Service | Monthly Cost | Features |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Simple Passive Cashflow | ~$10/loan | Basic payment collection |
| GeekPay | ~$15/loan | Land investor focused |
| Nickel | ~$20/loan | Full servicing |

## Closing Checklist

### Before Closing
☐ Purchase agreement signed by all parties
☐ Title search completed (clear or issues resolved)
☐ All liens paid or negotiated
☐ Closing documents prepared
☐ Funds verified/available
☐ Closing date scheduled
☐ Notary arranged (mobile if needed)

### At Closing
☐ Verify seller identity (photo ID)
☐ Collect all signatures
☐ Notarize deed and any other required documents
☐ Exchange funds (wire or cashier's check)
☐ Provide copies of all documents to all parties

### After Closing
☐ Record deed with county (same day if possible)
☐ Obtain recorded deed copy
☐ File closing documents
☐ Update your property inventory
☐ Begin disposition marketing

## Key Takeaways

1. **Match closing method to deal size** — Don't pay $1,000 to close a $2,000 property
2. **Know your state requirements** — Attorney states cost more; factor into offers
3. **Build relationships** — Find 1-2 land-friendly closers per target market
4. **Title insurance is cheap protection** — Consider for any deal over $5K
5. **Seller financing needs professional help** — Don't DIY complex deals
