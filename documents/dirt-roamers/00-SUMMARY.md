### Buyer Financing Strategies 💰
*Added Feb 12, 2026*

**Seller financing = sell at 100-120% MV + collect 8-12% interest**

Key to making this work safely:

**Optimal Structure:**
| Component | Recommended | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Down Payment | 20-30% | Buyer has skin in game, covers your costs |
| Interest Rate | 10% | Standard for land, justified vs banks |
| Amortization | 20-25 years | Lower payments = more buyers |
| Balloon | 3-7 years | Limits exposure, forces refinance/sale |

**Document Types:**
- **Land Contract** (smaller deals): You keep title until paid off, easier to reclaim
- **Deed of Trust** (larger deals): Buyer gets title, you get lien + power of sale

**Protection Checklist:**
✅ 20-30% down minimum
✅ Balloon payment (3-7 years)
✅ Power of sale clause (non-judicial foreclosure)
✅ Insurance requirement (you as loss payee)
✅ Due-on-sale clause
✅ Property tax verification annually

**Example Deal:**
- Buy 20 acres for $80K (financed at 80% = $64K note)
- Sell for $120K with 25% down = $30K
- Finance $90K at 11%, 7-year balloon
- Monthly: $866 income - $520 to your seller = **$346/mo net**
- Balloon year 7: Collect $82K, pay off $35K = **$47K net**
- **Total profit: ~$90K on $16K invested**

**State foreclosure timelines (Mateo's markets):**
- GA: ~45 days (fastest)
- AL, TN: ~90 days
- MT, NC: ~120-150 days
- FL, SC: 6-12 months (judicial)

**When to use:** Buyer can't get bank financing, you can command 100-120% MV, and higher total return justifies longer exit.

### Tax Optimization Guide 💰
*Added Feb 13, 2026*

**Critical insight:** Land flippers are classified as "real estate dealers" by IRS — profits taxed as ordinary income + 15.3% self-employment tax (not capital gains).

**Tax burden example (sole proprietor):**
- $100K profit → ~$39,300 in federal taxes (39.3%)
- Plus state income tax where applicable

**Entity Structure Comparison:**

| Structure | Best For | Tax Savings |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Sole Proprietor** | Testing (1-2 deals) | $0 (baseline) |
| **Single-Member LLC** | Liability protection | $0 tax savings (same as sole prop) |
| **LLC as S-Corp** | 5+ deals, $100K+ profit | $7,000-15,000/year |

**S-Corp Strategy:**
- Pay yourself reasonable W-2 salary (~50% of profit)
- Take remaining as distributions (no self-employment tax)
- Savings on $100K profit: ~$7,650/year

**Key Deductible Expenses:**
- Purchase costs, closing, due diligence (COGS)
- Marketing & advertising
- Data tools (Land Portal, skip tracing)
- CRM subscriptions (GoHighLevel)
- Mileage ($0.70/mile for 2026)
- Home office (if dedicated space)
- Professional services (CPA, attorney)
- Education & training

**Quarterly Estimated Tax Deadlines:**
- Q1: April 15
- Q2: June 16
- Q3: September 15
- Q4: January 15 (following year)

**Tax Planning Priorities:**

| Phase | Action Items |
|-------|-------------|
| **Starting Out** | Separate bank account, track expenses, set aside 40% for taxes |
| **Getting Serious** | Form LLC, hire real estate CPA, pay quarterly estimates |
| **Scaling** | Elect S-Corp, set up payroll, retirement contributions |

**State tax advantages (Mateo's markets):**
- ✅ FL, TN: No state income tax
- AL: 2-5% (low)
- NC: 4.5% flat (moderate)
- MT: 6.75% flat
- GA, SC: 5.75-6.5% (higher)

**Critical mistake to avoid:** Not paying quarterly taxes → underpayment penalties can be $1,000s.

**CPA recommendation:** Work with real estate dealer specialist (not just rental property CPA). Cost: $1,500-5,000/year. Typical ROI: $5,000-15,000 in additional deductions and strategies.

### Deal Analysis & Underwriting 📊
*Added Feb 15, 2026*

**Complete guide to comping vacant land and calculating offers.**

**The 5-Step Comping Process:**
1. Define property profile (acreage, zoning, access, utilities)
2. Search for comparable sales (Redfin, Zillow, County GIS)
3. Filter for true comparables (match key attributes)
4. Calculate price per acre with adjustments
5. Determine market value range

**Land MAO Formula:**
- **Cash MAO** = Market Value × 40-50%
- **Seller Finance MAO** = Market Value × 80-90%

**Adjustment Framework:**

| Factor | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| Paved road vs. gravel | +10-15% |
| Power at lot | +$3,000-5,000 |
| Water available | +$2,000-5,000 |
| Flood zone | -30-50% |
| Landlocked | -40-60% |

**Exit Strategy Profit Projections (Buy at 50% MV):**

| Exit Type | Sale Price | Profit Margin |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Quick flip @ 80% MV | Lower | 60% ROI |
| Retail @ 95% MV | Medium | 90% ROI |
| Seller finance @ 110% MV | Highest | 120%+ ROI |

**10-Point Deal Scorecard included** — Rate each deal on title, access, zoning, flood zone, topography, utilities, location, market activity, seller motivation, and price vs. MV.

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- Overestimating value (using asking prices, not sold prices)
- Ignoring access issues (landlocked kills deals)
- Not accounting for all costs
- Overpaying for "potential"
- Skipping due diligence

**Full deal analysis worksheet template included** for documenting comps, calculating offers, projecting exits, and making go/no-go decisions.

### Land Wholesaling & JV Partnerships 🤝
*Added Feb 19, 2026*

**Capital-light strategy for deals where you don't want to (or can't) buy outright.**

**Two Exit Options:**

| Method | When to Use | Spread Visibility |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Assignment** | Spreads <$10K, cooperative sellers | Visible to all parties |
| **Double Close** | Spreads >$10K, discretion needed | Hidden |

**Assignment:** Sign contract with "and/or assigns" clause → Find buyer → Assign contract rights for fee → Buyer closes directly with seller.

**Double Close:** Sign contract → Find buyer → Close A→B and B→C same day → You briefly take title → Profit hidden from seller.

**JV Partnership Structures:**
| Your Contribution | Typical Split |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Deal only | 25-40% |
| Deal + management | 40-50% |
| Deal + capital | 60-75% |

**Legal Requirements:**
- Add "and/or assigns" to all PSAs
- Disclose investor/wholesaler status upfront
- Use investor-friendly title companies
- Market the contract, not the property (if not owner yet)
- Get JV agreements reviewed by attorney

**When to Wholesale (vs. buy):**
- Insufficient capital for purchase
- Testing new market with minimal risk
- Deal doesn't fit seller financing criteria
- Need quick cash over long-term returns

**Application for Dirt Roamers:**
